# Policy brief / 政策简报

## Reader and purpose

The reader is a decision-maker who needs a defensible diagnosis, options and an actionable recommendation.

## Expected functions

- executive summary;
- policy problem and affected groups;
- evidence and current conditions;
- options and trade-offs;
- recommendation;
- implementation, responsibilities, risks and monitoring;
- methods or source note when necessary.

## Review priorities

1. Distinguish observed facts, analytical inference and normative recommendation.
2. State jurisdiction, time, population and institutional scope.
3. Compare at least the plausible alternatives, including the status quo where relevant.
4. Name implementing actors, instruments, sequence and monitoring indicators.
5. Do not hide distributional effects, uncertainty or implementation risk.
6. Use concise headings that answer decision questions.

## Register

Use restrained institutional language. Concrete verbs and accountable actors are preferred, but established policy terms must remain stable.

## Hard blocks

- unsupported legal or policy claims;
- invented statistics, consultation results or stakeholder positions;
- removing eligibility, jurisdiction or uncertainty qualifiers;
- presenting the model's analysis process as policy reasoning in the brief.
