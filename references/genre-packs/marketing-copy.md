# Marketing copy

## Reader and purpose

The reader is evaluating whether a product, service or idea solves a relevant problem. Persuasion must remain supportable.

## Review priorities

1. Name the audience and their concrete problem.
2. State the value proposition in plain language.
3. Connect features to believable outcomes.
4. Use proof, examples or mechanisms instead of unsupported superlatives.
5. Give one clear next action.
6. Keep brand voice consistent across headline, body and CTA.

## Allowed style

Energy, compression and memorable language are allowed. They do not justify fabricated urgency, false scarcity, fake testimonials or unverifiable comparisons.

## Hard blocks

- invented customers, testimonials, rankings or performance numbers;
- absolute claims without support;
- implying regulatory, medical, financial or legal guarantees;
- copying another brand's distinctive wording;
- process narration or model commentary in published copy.
