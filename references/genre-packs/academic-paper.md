# Academic paper

## Reader and purpose

The reader is a skeptical scholar or reviewer. The text must make claims inspectable and show how evidence supports them.

## Expected structure

Follow the target venue. Without a supplied template, preserve the manuscript's current disciplinary structure rather than forcing an ML-paper outline.

## Review priorities

1. Research question, contribution, method and evidence are aligned.
2. Every important claim has a citation, result or explicit theoretical argument.
3. Sample, time, method and uncertainty qualifiers remain attached to the claim they limit.
4. Related work names real sources and does not manufacture novelty through caricature.
5. Abstract and conclusion do not exceed the evidence in the body.
6. Meta-review, submission strategy and editing instructions stay outside the manuscript.

## Register rules

- Technical terms are not “jargon” when the field needs them.
- Passive voice can be appropriate in methods; do not ban it mechanically.
- First person depends on venue and discipline.
- Do not make formal prose conversational merely to vary rhythm.

## Hard blocks

- invented citations, results or methods;
- model reasoning or reviewer instructions in the manuscript;
- changed numbers, units, p-values, confidence intervals or sample sizes;
- deleted limitations or uncertainty.
