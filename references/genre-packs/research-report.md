# Research report

## Reader and purpose

The reader needs a reliable account of the question, evidence, analysis, findings, limitations and implications.

## Expected functions

- executive summary or abstract;
- background and research question;
- scope, definitions, data and methods;
- findings and analysis;
- limitations;
- conclusions and recommendations where authorized;
- sources and appendices.

## Review priorities

1. Define record counts, unique entities, samples and denominators precisely.
2. Separate source data from inference and recommendation.
3. Trace major claims to tables, figures, records or citations.
4. Keep methods and limitations visible enough to judge reliability.
5. Do not let polished prose hide missing evidence.
6. Ensure summaries use the same numbers and scope as the body.

## Hard blocks

- invented records, sources or analysis;
- inconsistent definitions or denominators;
- internal file paths and tool logs in the report body;
- conclusions broader than the verified evidence.
