# Executive memo

## Reader and purpose

The reader needs to decide, approve, intervene or understand a risk quickly.

## Preferred order

1. Decision or current status;
2. why it matters now;
3. evidence and constraints;
4. options and trade-offs;
5. recommendation, owner and next checkpoint.

## Review priorities

- Put the requested decision near the top.
- Quantify impact when verified.
- Separate known facts, assumptions and unresolved questions.
- Name owners and dates for agreed actions.
- Keep background proportional to the decision.
- Avoid presentation-style headings that say little.

## Hard blocks

- hiding material risk or uncertainty;
- inventing alignment, approval or ownership;
- changing financial, legal or operational facts;
- chat handoffs and internal editing notes in the memo.
