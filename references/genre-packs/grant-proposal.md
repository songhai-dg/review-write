# Grant proposal

## Reader and purpose

The reader is an expert panel deciding whether the proposed work is important, credible, feasible and aligned with the call.

## Expected functions

- problem and significance;
- objectives or research questions;
- current foundation and evidence;
- innovation relative to existing work;
- approach, milestones and evaluation;
- team capability and risk management;
- expected outputs and impact;
- direct compliance with the funder's required headings and limits.

## Review priorities

1. Objectives are specific enough to evaluate.
2. Innovation is a verifiable distinction, not praise words.
3. Preliminary evidence is clearly separated from proposed work.
4. Work packages connect to milestones, responsibilities and success criteria.
5. Risks have credible mitigations.
6. Claims about policy, industry or social impact identify a plausible path.

## Protected features

Preserve call terminology, eligibility wording, required headings, budget facts, team roles, dates, technical terms and cited preliminary results.

## Hard blocks

- fabricating preliminary results, partnerships, facilities or team experience;
- changing funder language or compliance commitments;
- turning a proposal into promotional copy;
- placing reviewer strategy, model instructions or TODOs in the application body.
